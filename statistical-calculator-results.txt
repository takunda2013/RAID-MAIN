1	Results

This chapter presents the results for the Statistical-Calculator component using evidence from:

- `Statistical-Calculator/code/feature_extractor.py`
- `Statistical-Calculator/code/FEATURE_EXTRACTOR_README.md`
- `XGBoost Classifier/probability_audit_fixes/outputs/metrics_summary.csv`
- `XGBoost Classifier/probability_audit_fixes/outputs/feature_importance_xgb_statistical_gain.csv`
- `Deployment/test_essay_performance.json`

The statistical calculator is evaluated as a 10-feature text signal module (surprisal and derivatives), both as a standalone feature view and as an auditor-facing subsystem.

1.1	Introduction:

This chapter addresses three statistical-calculator research questions:

- RQ1: How predictive are the 10 statistical features on their own?
- RQ2: How much does probability calibration improve statistical-only models?
- RQ3: Is statistical-only modeling suitable for explanation/audit use (less probability saturation)?

The component computes the following 10 features per essay:

- `mean_surprisal`, `stdev_surprisal`, `var_surprisal`, `skew_surprisal`, `kurtosis_surprisal`
- `mean_diff_surprisal`, `stdev_diff_surprisal`
- `var_second_diff_loglik`, `entropy_second_diff_loglik`, `autocorr_second_diff_loglik`

Evaluation protocol (from `run_config.json`):

- Train split: 44,867 rows (`unseen`)
- Calibration split: 6,083 rows (`val`)
- Final test split: 6,087 rows (`test`)

Models evaluated on the statistical view only:

- `xgb_statistical` (XGBoost, 10 features)
- `logistic_statistical` (Logistic Regression, 10 features)

Each was tested with `raw`, `sigmoid`, and `isotonic` probabilities.

1.2	Presentation of Findings:

1.2.1	XGBoost Statistical-Only Performance

| Variant | Accuracy | AUC | AI Precision | AI Recall | AI F1 | Brier | ECE | middle_10_90 |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| xgb_statistical/raw | 0.5780 | 0.8623 | 0.9685 | 0.4402 | 0.6053 | 0.2317 | 0.2708 | 1.0000 |
| xgb_statistical/sigmoid | 0.8216 | 0.8623 | 0.8495 | 0.9204 | 0.8835 | 0.1331 | 0.0657 | 0.6223 |
| xgb_statistical/isotonic | 0.8216 | 0.8638 | 0.8495 | 0.9204 | 0.8835 | 0.1262 | 0.0122 | 0.4952 |

Confusion behavior for `xgb_statistical/isotonic`:

- TN=882, FP=730, FN=356, TP=4119
- Human recall (specificity) = 54.71%
- AI recall = 92.04%
- Balanced accuracy = 73.38%

Interpretation:

- Raw XGBoost statistical probabilities are poorly calibrated for a 0.5 threshold (high FN count).
- Calibration dramatically improves utility (especially Brier and ECE), while preserving ranking power (AUC remains ~0.86).
- Even after calibration, the model remains AI-sensitive but human-weaker than semantic models.

1.2.2	Logistic Statistical-Only Performance

| Variant | Accuracy | AUC | AI Precision | AI Recall | AI F1 | Brier | ECE | middle_10_90 |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| logistic_statistical/raw | 0.8461 | 0.9539 | 0.9802 | 0.8069 | 0.8852 | 0.1162 | 0.1522 | 0.3660 |
| logistic_statistical/sigmoid | 0.8929 | 0.9539 | 0.9078 | 0.9508 | 0.9288 | 0.0770 | 0.0354 | 0.3240 |
| logistic_statistical/isotonic | 0.8949 | 0.9527 | 0.9236 | 0.9343 | 0.9289 | 0.0755 | 0.0151 | 0.3105 |

Confusion behavior for `logistic_statistical/isotonic`:

- TN=1266, FP=346, FN=294, TP=4181
- Human recall = 78.54%
- AI recall = 93.43%
- Balanced accuracy = 85.98%

Interpretation:

- Logistic statistical models substantially outperform XGBoost statistical models on this dataset.
- `logistic_statistical/isotonic` is the strongest statistical-only configuration overall.

1.2.3	Calibration Impact (Key Quantitative Changes)

A) `xgb_statistical` raw -> isotonic

- Accuracy: `0.5780 -> 0.8216` (+24.36 percentage points)
- Brier score: `0.2317 -> 0.1262` (45.53% reduction)
- ECE: `0.2708 -> 0.0122` (95.49% reduction)
- `middle_10_90`: `1.0000 -> 0.4952` (less uncertainty spread after calibration)

B) `logistic_statistical` raw -> isotonic

- Accuracy: `0.8461 -> 0.8949` (+4.88 percentage points)
- Brier score: `0.1162 -> 0.0755` (35.03% reduction)
- ECE: `0.1522 -> 0.0151` (90.08% reduction)
- `middle_10_90`: `0.3660 -> 0.3105` (still retains meaningful probability movement)

Result:

- Calibration is essential for statistical-only reliability.
- Isotonic calibration provided the best overall statistical-only probability quality.

1.2.4	Feature Signal Strength (XGBoost Gain)

Top gain-ranked statistical features (`feature_importance_xgb_statistical_gain.csv`):

1. `mean_surprisal` (5273.06)
2. `stdev_surprisal` (4796.27)
3. `var_surprisal` (4375.58)
4. `var_second_diff_loglik` (3562.01)
5. `stdev_diff_surprisal` (3451.47)

Lower-use features include `skew_surprisal`, `mean_diff_surprisal`, and `kurtosis_surprisal`.
In this run, `entropy_second_diff_loglik` and `autocorr_second_diff_loglik` did not appear among non-zero gain splits, indicating weak contribution under XGBoost statistical-only training.

1.2.5	Operational Result in Deployment Path

From `Deployment/test_essay_performance.json` on a 571-word essay:

- Statistical feature extraction time: 2,286.754 ms (~2.287 s)
- Extracted all 10 features successfully
- Example output values:
  - `mean_surprisal`: 3.1071
  - `stdev_surprisal`: 2.6415
  - `var_surprisal`: 6.9777
  - `entropy_second_diff_loglik`: 2.4072
  - `autocorr_second_diff_loglik`: -0.6601

This confirms the statistical calculator is functioning end-to-end in deployment and producing complete feature vectors for downstream audit prediction.

1.3	Conclusion:

The statistical calculator provides meaningful discriminative signal, but not production-level accuracy when used alone. Its strongest standalone configuration was `logistic_statistical/isotonic` (Accuracy 89.49%, AUC 0.9527, ECE 0.0151), while XGBoost statistical-only required calibration to become usable.

Most importantly, the statistical pathway produces less saturated probabilities than semantic-heavy models, which makes it valuable for explanation workflows. Therefore, the statistical calculator is best positioned as an auditor/explainability component rather than the primary final classifier in the current system.
