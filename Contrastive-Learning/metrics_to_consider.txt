Great question. Here are the metrics that would make a strong research contribution for your specific task:

---

**Core Detection Metrics**

These are your primary results table:

```
Binary F1          → overall human vs AI detection
Binary AUC-ROC     → threshold-independent detection quality
Precision          → of essays flagged as AI, how many actually are
Recall             → of all AI essays, how many were caught
```

---

**Adversarial Robustness Metrics**
These are your most novel contribution — no standard AI detector paper focuses here:

```
Label 2 recall           → paraphrased AI caught rate
Detection gap            → label 1 recall - label 2 recall
                           (measures how much paraphrasing hurts)
Paraphraser robustness   → accuracy broken down per paraphrasing model
                           (model A vs B vs C separately)
```

The detection gap is particularly powerful:
```
Naive classifier:    gap = 0.40  (badly fooled by paraphrasing)
Your model:          gap = 0.08  (robust to paraphrasing)
```
That contrast tells the whole story of why contrastive learning helps.

---

**Embedding Quality Metrics**
These validate the contrastive learning stage itself:

```
Linear probe AUC         → already in your script
Silhouette score         → how well separated the 3 clusters are
Intra-class distance     → how tight label 1 and 2 cluster together
                           (validates pulling them together worked)
Inter-class distance     → how far human vs AI clusters are
t-SNE visualization      → qualitative but powerful for a paper figure
```

---

**Comparison Baselines**
Essential for a research paper — you need to show your model beats something:

```
GPTZero / Originality.ai     → commercial detectors
Perplexity-based detector    → traditional statistical approach
Fine-tuned RoBERTa           → same model WITHOUT contrastive learning
                               (ablation — proves contrastive helps)
```

That last one is your **ablation study** — same architecture, same data, but trained with cross-entropy only. If your contrastive model beats it on label 2 recall, you've proven your contribution.

---

**Cross-Domain Generalization**
If you have essays from different sources/domains:

```
In-domain F1      → essays similar to training data
Out-of-domain F1  → essays from unseen domains/styles
Generalization gap → difference between the two
```

---

**The metrics that will make your paper stand out:**

```
1. Detection gap (label 1 vs label 2 recall)
2. Per-paraphraser breakdown (model A, B, C separately)
3. Ablation vs non-contrastive baseline
4. t-SNE showing embedding clusters
```
